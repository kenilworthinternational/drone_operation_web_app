# LEAVE

## Leave Definition

"Leave" means any period during which an employee is permitted to absent himself from duty with approval.

## Classification of Leave Provisions

Leave/days off are classified as:

- Annual leave
- Casual leave
- Sick/Medical leave
- Short leave
- Duty leave
- Bulk leave
- Maternity leave
- Paternity leave
- Lieu leave
- No-Pay leave
- Work-from-home

## Leave Entitlement - During 1st Year of Employment

### Casual Leave

During the first year of employment (i.e. from 01st January to 31st December) the leave entitlement will be one (01) day of casual leave for every two months work.

### Sick Leave

During the first year of employment (i.e. from 01st January to 31st December) the leave entitlement will be one (01) day of sick leave for every two months work.

Sick leave must be supported by a medical certificate from a registered medical practitioner, where:

- The period of absence from work exceeds two days (02) or
- Where the absence precedes or follows any period of casual leave, statutory holiday or annual leave.

## Leave Entitlement - After the 1st Year

### Annual Leave

Confirmed employees are entitled to Annual Leave from the 2nd calendar year of employment and the entitlement is as follows:

| Quarter joined (in preceding year) | Entitlement of Annual Leave in 2nd year |
| --- | --- |
| 1st Quarter (1st January - 31st March) | 14 days |
| 2nd Quarter (1st April - 30th June) | 10 days |
| 3rd Quarter (1st July - 30th September) | 7 days |
| 4th Quarter (1st October - 31st December) | 4 days |

In subsequent years, employees will be entitled to fourteen (14) days of Annual Leave. Weekends, Poya Days, Gazetted Mercantile Holidays will be excluded when calculating the leave entitlement of an employee. Annual leave by its nature must be preferably applied for in advance. It must be taken on days mutually agreed upon by the employer and employee.

A period of leave of not less than seven (07) consecutive days, excluding holidays, should be granted to all employees entitled to vacation leave.

### Casual Leave

Permanent employees are entitled to seven (07) days casual leave per year, which can be utilized for private business or ill health if the sick leave has been fully utilized. Such leave can be availed in the following manner:

- Employees are requested to give adequate notice prior to taking leave as far as possible.
- For private business or for any other reason including ill health where sick leave entitlement has been exhausted.
- Not more than 2 days of casual leave to be taken together at any one time.
- No entitlement to causal leave exists on any day preceding or following annual leave.

### Sick Leave

Permanent employees are entitled to seven (07) days sick leave per year, which can be utilized for ill health. Sick leave must be supported by a medical certificate from a registered medical practitioner, where:

- The period of absence from work exceeds two days (02) or
- Where the absence precedes or follows any period of casual leave, statutory or customary holiday or annual leave.

### Short Leave

Short leave is given at the discretion of the HOD. However, the maximum period of short leave allowed at any one time is one and a half hours (1 & 1/2 hrs) and the maximum period of short leave available per month is three hours (3hrs), which could be taken in the following manner:

- Beginning of the day
- Before a meal interval or at the end of the meal interval
- Before the end of the day
- Not more than two (02) short leaves are permitted per month.

It should be noted that there is no provision for short leave under any law and it is granted by the Management only as a concession at the employer's discretion and not on the basis of an entitlement.

### Duty leave

Duty leave is an authorized paid absence from work that enables employees to complete compulsory duty/service. It could be an employee's participation at an event, meeting, field visit, training/seminar, forum, election, court presence on behalf of the organization, etc.

### Bulk Leave

The Bulk Leave allows employees to work for an extended period without taking regular weekly rest days and subsequently take a proportionate number of days off. This arrangement is designed to provide flexibility while ensuring that work commitments are met without disruption to operations.

Employees opting for bulk leave must complete the agreed upon extended work period before availing themselves of the corresponding leave. For example, an employee who works for 24 consecutive days may take 6 days off as bulk leave.

This arrangement requires prior approval from HOD.

### Maternity Leave

All female employees shall be granted eighty-four (84) days maternity leave on full pay and it excludes the Two Weekly holidays, Poya days and Statutory Mercantile Holidays.

The eighty-four (84) working days can be taken up to fourteen (14) days pre-confinement leave (including the day the confinement) and seventy (70) days post-confinement leave. Notification of date of commencement of maternity leave has to be notified by the applicant and pre-authority taken prior to taking maternity leave.

Combination of medical leave with annual leave could be allowed only with prior permission from the Managing Director.

### Miscarriage

Leave on account of a miscarriage shall not be considered as maternity leave, but as medical leave, save and except that there is a viable foetus.

Forty-two (42) days leave is granted to a female employee if the confinement does not result in the issue of live child and it excludes the Two Weekly holidays, Poya days and Statutory Mercantile Holidays.

The forty-two (42) working days can be taken up to fourteen (14) days pre-confinement leave (including the day the confinement) and twenty-eight (28) days post-confinement leave. Notification of date of commencement of maternity leave has to be notified by the applicant and pre-authority taken prior to taking maternity leave.

### Nursing intervals

A female employee who is nursing a child under one (1) year of age shall be granted nursing intervals within the normal working day in the following manner:

- Where a creche or other suitable place is provided for by the employer, two (2) nursing intervals each with a minimum period of thirty (30) minutes.
- Where a creche or other suitable place is not provided for by the employer, two (2) nursing intervals each with a minimum period of one (01) hour.

The abovementioned nursing intervals should be allowed at such times the female employee requires as following:

- If working from office - 2 hours in the morning or 2 hours in the evening or 1 hour in the morning and 1 hour in the evening
- If working from home - 1 hour in the morning or 1 hour in the evening or 30 minutes in the morning and 30 minutes in the evening

The nursing intervals shall be allowed in addition to any interval for meals or rest allowed for such employee and shall be regarded as time worked within her employment.

### Paternity Leave

All male employees shall be granted five (05) days paternity leave on full pay and it excludes the Two Weekly holidays, Poya days and Statutory Holidays.

This leave is a temporary absence from work intended to support employed fathers during the birth of their child, adoption of a child, or the arrival of a baby through a surrogacy arrangement.

### Lieu Leave (For Executives ONLY)

Lieu leave is granted at the discretion of the HOD as a means to encourage employees to take time off in recognition of additional hours worked, without negatively affecting overall work performance.

Employees are expected to manage their work hours responsibly and should not accumulate an excessive number of extra hours. As a general guideline, no more than two working days of lieu leave may be accrued in any given month, unless there are exceptional circumstances and prior approval has been obtained from the HOD.

Lieu leave should be utilized within 30 days of being earned. If not taken within this period, the lieu leave for that month will be forfeited.

Please note that lieu leave is not a statutory entitlement. It is provided solely at the discretion of Management as a concession and does not constitute a legal right.

### Accident Leave

If an employee sustains an injury as a result of an accident arising out of and during the course of their employment, they may be entitled to Accident Leave in accordance with applicable labour laws and the company's policy provided that the incident did not occur due to the employee's own wilful misconduct or negligence.

Employees will not be eligible for paid Accident Leave or compensation under the Workmen's Compensation Ordinance if the accident is found to have resulted from their wilful default or negligence.

#### Eligibility

Employees will be entitled to Accident Leave if they meet the following conditions:

- The employee is disabled due to an accident that occurred while performing official duties in the service of the organization.
- An investigation confirms that the accident was not caused by wilful default or negligence on the part of the employee.
- The leave is recommended by a qualified MBBS medical doctor based on the employee's medical condition.

#### Entitlement

There is no limit on the number of days of accident leave, provided that all conditions mentioned above are met.

The duration and benefits of accident leave, including medical coverage, will be determined based on the severity of the injury and legal provisions.

Employees must report the accident immediately to their supervisor/ HOD and the HR department, along with the necessary medical documentation, to process the leave and associated benefits.

### No-Pay Leave

No-pay leave is granted without pay for the day(s) an employee is absent. It is typically approved at the employee's request and is subject to the discretion of the HOD. Taking no-pay leave may impact the employee's performance evaluation and other related entitlements.

### Work Off-Site / Working-from-home

The Work Off-Site / Working-from-Home is granted to allow employees to perform their duties remotely without compromising on the expected work output. This arrangement is designed to be mutually beneficial to both the employer and the employee.

Employees working off-site must ensure that they fulfil their work responsibilities within the standard work hours. A full workday off-site requires the completion of nine hours and thirty minutes (9 1/2) of work, while a half-day requires five (5) hours of work.

This arrangement should be managed with prior approval from the employee's manager or HOD. Working from home or other off-site locations should not negatively impact the employee's performance or other work-related responsibilities.
